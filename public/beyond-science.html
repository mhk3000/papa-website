My Prophet Mohammed

Beyond Science

Posted on Fri, Sep 6, 2024 Duat Mutlaqeen Mohammed Burhanuddin Observation

A Life-Changing Miracle

This account, narrated by Shabbir Bhai Ujjaiy, recounts an incident involving Syedna Burhanuddin AQ, the 52nd Dai al Fatemi of the Shia Mustaeli Dawoodi Bohra community. At the age of 19, he faced a distressing situation when his 15-year-old sister, Ruksana, was unable to urinate. After consulting Dr. Kaizar Dhansura, he took her to Prince Aga Khan Hospital, where the doctor advised dialysis, a rare procedure at that time. He then visited Raudat Tahera, where Syedna Burhanuddin was present. After hearing his story, Syedna asked him to rename his sister Fatema. Upon returning to the hospital, Ruksana urinated for the first time, much to the astonishment of the doctor, who subsequently discharged her. This event strengthened their faith and gratitude towards Allah.

Divine Intervention

In another narration by Shabbir Bhai Ujjainy, he shares a life-altering experience involving Syedi Mazoon Saheb, Qutbuddin. One night, he was returning home from Badri Mahal when he was prompted to do charity a second time by Syedi Mazoon Saheb. While waiting for a bus, he chose to walk to VT station instead. During the journey, he encountered a severe bus accident but found himself miraculously unharmed, while others around him were injured. The next morning, he recounted his experience to Syedi Mazoon, who indicated that his charitable act had saved his life. This revelation profoundly impacted his devotion, leading to over 46 years of service.

Restoration of Voice

This account tells of Syedna Burhanuddin Maula's visit to Surat for a religious assembly. A father approached Syedna with his 12-year-old daughter, who was unable to speak. Upon hearing the girl's condition, Syedna prompted her to respond affirmatively, to which she was able to say "yes." With this, Syedna miraculously restored her ability to speak and hear, demonstrating the power of faith and divine intervention.

A Miracle of Healing

This narration describes an experience shared by a childhood friend of Hunaid Khorakiwala. During a visit to the USA, his friend brought his son, who was unable to walk, to Syedna Burhanuddin. After examining the child and removing the doctor's bindings, Syedna prayed for him. In the following days, the boy began to show movement, and ultimately, he was able to walk. The joy of this miracle left the father in tears as he witnessed his son's transformation.

The Gift of Parenthood

In this account, Shabbir Bhai Ujjayini recalls a woman who approached Syedna Taher Saifuddin, expressing her deep desire for a child. Syedna instructed her to perform a "chalisa" (a prayer performed 40 times). The woman diligently visited Syedna for 40 days, and upon completing her prayers, she experienced the miracle of motherhood. Despite complications after childbirth, she took her child to Syedna, requesting the return of her son. In an incredible turn of events, her child, previously unresponsive, began to cry and move as they left Syedna's presence.

Blessings of Inauguration

In another narration, a believer approached Syedna Taher Saifuddin for the inauguration of his factory. After several delays, Syedna instructed that the inauguration should be conducted by his successor, Syedna Mohammed Burhanuddin. Upon attending the inauguration, the believer expressed gratitude to Syedna for guiding him, acknowledging that blessings often come through divine intermediaries.

The Wisdom of Adam

This account discusses the creation of Adam AS. The Lord created Adam from the earth and endowed him with knowledge of the holy names, leading the angels to prostrate to him. Iblis, however, refused, resulting in his expulsion from the garden of bliss. Adam and Eve's subsequent actions led to their own expulsion, but they were forgiven upon taking the recommendation of five holy names. This narrative raises profound questions about divine justice and the wisdom behind the actions of both the Lord and Adam.

The Miracle of Divine Guidance

This account is narrated by Mulla Shabbir Bhai Ujjainy about an interaction with Syedi Mazoon Maula Qutbuddin during Ashara vaez in Karachi. A believer approached Mazoon Maula for a name for his new business but received no immediate response. However, after returning home and praying for guidance, he received confirmation of the name from Syedna Burhanuddin through Mazoon Maula. This timely response demonstrated the profound connection between the believers and their spiritual leaders.

A Lesson in Intention

In this account, a woman presented herself to Syedna Taher Saifuddin for Qadambosi, holding two one-rupee coins. After performing her salaam, Syedna accepted only one coin, illustrating the significance of intention over action. The woman explained her reasoning for bringing two coins, and Syedna acknowledged her true intention, reinforcing the understanding that Allah considers the heart's intent above material offerings.

Reuniting Family Bonds

In this narrative, a woman seeking the blessings of Syedna Taher Saifuddin for a child was guided to visit her brother instead of returning home. Despite a strained relationship, her visit prompted reconciliation, leading to familial harmony. This not only fulfilled her wish for a child but also restored long-lost connections within the family, showcasing the transformative power of divine guidance.
